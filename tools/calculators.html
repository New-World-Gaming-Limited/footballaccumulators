Which Calculator Do You Need?

If you are betting on a football accumulator, start with the accumulator calculator. Enter the decimal odds for each leg and your stake, and it tells you the exact return if all selections win.

The odds converter is useful when a bookmaker shows fractional odds but you prefer decimal, or when you want to compare prices across international sites that use American moneyline odds.

Matched bettors should use the lay bet calculator to work out the correct lay stake and liability for any qualifying or free bet offer. The arbitrage calculator shows whether a genuine sure bet exists across two or three bookmakers, and tells you exactly how much to stake on each outcome.

For football golf, horse racing and other each-way markets, the each-way calculator handles any place fraction (1/4, 1/5, 1/8) and finish position combination.
