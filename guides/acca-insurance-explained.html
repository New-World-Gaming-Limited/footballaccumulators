Acca insurance, properly explained

Home/Guides/Acca insurance, properly explained

Acca insurance is one of the most popular promotions offered by football bookmakers. The idea is simple: if one leg of your accumulator loses and all others win, you get your stake back - usually as a free bet. It sounds like a small edge but over dozens of accas, it makes a real difference.

How Acca Insurance Works

When you opt into an acca insurance promotion and your acca has exactly one losing selection, the bookmaker refunds your stake. The refund is typically in the form of a free bet rather than cash, but it gives you another chance to win.

Most acca insurance deals require a minimum number of legs (usually four or five) and a minimum combined odds threshold (often 2/1 or greater). Some bookmakers also cap the maximum stake eligible for insurance.

Which Bookmakers Offer Acca Insurance?

Most major bookmakers offer some form of acca insurance on football. The exact terms vary, so always check the small print before placing your acca. Look for how the refund is paid (cash vs free bet), any minimum odds requirements, and whether the insurance applies automatically or needs to be opted into each time.

Acca Boosts

Separate from insurance, many bookmakers also offer acca boosts - an enhancement to your winnings when your acca lands. A 10% acca boost on a 10 accumulator returning 80 would pay out 88 instead. These are worth factoring in when choosing which bookmaker to use for a particular acca.

Making the Most of Acca Promotions

The key is to know which bookmaker is running the best promotion for your bet type. A four-fold acca might attract insurance at one bookmaker and a boost at another - choose based on your acca and the likely return. Keep bookmaker accounts at multiple operators so you have flexibility to pick the best deal.
